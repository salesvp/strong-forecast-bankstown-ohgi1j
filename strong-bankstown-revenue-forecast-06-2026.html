Bankstown closed May 2026 at 151 active members, generating approximately $36,400 gross revenue per month — modestly below the $40,000 breakeven line. The active base is recovering from a low of 120 (September 2025) and is tracking upward through June.

This forecast is presented in two parts. The primary forecast covers the immediate seven months to January 2027 — the period that matters most for near-term commitments. The longer-term outlook then extends the same trajectory through 2027 toward the $50,000 and $60,000 targets. All figures use a deliberately conservative basis, with pack and intro revenue set below their trailing averages and data-derived seasonal reductions applied to August and December.

Section 07
Notes & Assumptions
  • Baseline. Confirmed May 2026 close of 151 active members. June is tracking +6 mid-month, consistent with the assumed growth rate.
  • Window definition. The primary forecast runs seven months to January 2027. A six-month window would end in December, the seasonal trough; extending to January reflects the recovered position more fairly.
  • Seasonal adjustments. August net growth is set to zero and December to −15 members, derived from the average of the prior two years' actual movements (August +5 / −4; December −19 / −11).
  • Member-driven model. Revenue is projected from the active-member count at $215 per member, plus a fixed non-recurring floor. Member acquisition is the primary driver of all milestones.
  • Historical precedent. The studio carried 162–180 active members through 2024 and reached 203 total members in August 2024. The membership levels in this forecast have been achieved at this site previously.
  • Scope. All figures are gross revenue. Operating costs and rent are outside the scope of this projection. Figures are indicative and dependent on the stated assumptions.
